Mj Not Innocent

MJ Not Innocent is a critical resource dedicated to exploring the complex and troubling legacy of Michael Jackson, particularly his relationships with young boys and the allegations of sexual abuse that have surfaced over the years. This website goes beyond mere speculation; it aims to unpack the mountains of misinformation that have grown around Jackson's life, fuelled by his fervent fanbase, family members, and the Jackson estate.

Michael Jackson's behavior with young boys raises serious concerns. He undeniably spent hundreds, if not thousands, of nights alone with them behind closed doors. This behavior is viewed by many as highly questionable, yet there exists a dedicated group—often referred to as the cult of celebrity—who refuse to acknowledge any wrongdoing and instead glorify him at every turn. This idolization leads to an alarming dismissal of serious allegations and a culture of denial that can be damaging to actual victims of abuse.

The Internet is inundated with pro-Jackson misinformation and conspiracy theories. This distortion of facts isn't limited to a few misguided fans; it extends to Jackson's own family members and the estate that continues to promote his legacy. In some troubling instances, some individuals have claimed to be victims of sexual abuse themselves, not out of genuine experience but as a tactic to undermine the accusers of Michael Jackson. They often assert that those who accuse him cannot be real victims, claiming to know what "real victims" should look like and how they should behave.

This phenomenon reveals a concerning trend: the likelihood that many of these individuals are not actual victims of abuse, but instead are leveraging their supposed experiences to elevate their idol. In doing so, they risk degrading their own narratives and the serious matter of abuse itself, often out of a desire to glorify their favourite celebrity.

Since Michael Jackson's death in 2009, I have researched the allegations and the public's response to them. The level of hatred directed at those who have come forward with accusations against him is staggering. The situation intensified significantly following the release of Dan Reed's Leaving Neverland, which showcased the personal testimonies of James Safechuck and Wade Robson, bringing renewed attention to the allegations and further polarization among fans and critics alike.

Despite the abundance of articles written about Jackson, few dedicated websites exist that critically engage with his behaviour and defend against the prevailing pro-Jackson narrative. The Internet remains riddled with misinformation, even in widely used resources like Wikipedia, making it challenging to navigate the truth.
